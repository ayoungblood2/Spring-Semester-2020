  • history
    • this semester history provided me with context for design. instead of conceptualizing from personal ideation, my process became reversed in history of graphic design. i found myself working from historical references first, then contextualizing into my own design language.

  • interactive
    • we covered a lot in interactive this semester. i've been actively learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and experimenting ways to introduce these methods into my everyday studio practice. the readings we had about the internet and the relationship to embodied experience were eye-opening discussions. everything of what i thought web-design "should-be" vanished. all expectations and rules, gone. i learned there is a space for art in the web-space. i appreciate the freedom of expression and encouragement of creativity from conceptually-driven prompts in this class. i want to incorporate this new perspective of organizing and translating design/process into an interactive space.

  • printmaking
    • printmaking considers ways in which multiplicity, circulation, and archives have functioned within contemporary print based artworks. We had visits to on-campus collections, collaborative studio work, and in class discussions of pertinent texts aim to place the making of printed media within an expansive field of art practices. Although, the second part of the semester was to focus on how these concepts, materials, and forms connect to our own individual studio practices through projects and printshop demonstrations focusing on the stencil and the book (we still had discussions of what we'd like to pursue in the future).
